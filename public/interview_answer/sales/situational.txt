1) If a client refuses to sign a contract, I would first seek to understand their concerns clearly. This helps identify whether the issue is pricing, terms, or trust rather than forcing a decision. For example, I would ask targeted questions, address objections, and adjust the proposal if possible while protecting company interests.

2) I would handle competing sales opportunities by evaluating their potential value and urgency. This ensures time and effort are focused where they can generate the best results. For example, I would prioritize opportunities with higher conversion likelihood or strategic importance.

3) If I am behind my sales quota, I would review my pipeline and adjust my strategy immediately. This helps identify gaps and opportunities for improvement. For example, I would increase follow-ups with warm leads and refine my pitch to improve conversion rates.

4) I would approach a difficult client meeting by preparing thoroughly and staying calm. Preparation helps me address concerns confidently and professionally. For example, I would review the client’s history, anticipate objections, and focus on solutions rather than arguments.

5) To retain an unhappy client, I would listen carefully and acknowledge their concerns. This shows respect and helps rebuild trust. For example, I would apologize if appropriate, offer a solution, and follow up to ensure the issue was resolved.

6) I would prioritize urgent sales tasks by assessing deadlines and business impact. This helps ensure critical opportunities are not missed. For example, I would focus first on tasks that affect revenue or client commitments before less urgent activities.

7) If a client requests an impossible deal, I would explain limitations honestly and professionally. Transparency helps maintain credibility and trust. For example, I would propose alternative solutions that still meet the client’s core needs.

8) I would handle conflict between sales team members by encouraging open and respectful communication. This helps resolve misunderstandings and refocus the team on shared goals. For example, I would facilitate a discussion to clarify roles and agree on a common approach.

9) If a major deal falls through, I would analyze what went wrong and refocus quickly. Learning from the experience helps prevent similar issues in the future. For example, I would review feedback, update my pipeline, and pursue other opportunities.

10) I would approach upselling by identifying additional value for the client. This ensures the recommendation is relevant rather than forced. For example, I would suggest an upgrade or add-on that supports the client’s long-term goals.
